01 · Story

Story

Family recipes from Beirut,
one shared table.

Du Liban is the work of a family that learned to cook before they learned to write. What we serve every night is that memory: the mezze shared without haste, the grill lit at sundown, the orange-blossom sweets that close the meal.

"In every mezze there is a family story. We cook as in our grandmother's home in Beirut: with time, with hands and with memory."

The wood-fired oven, the stone mortar and the grill are the three pillars of our kitchen. From them come freshly baked manoucheh, the knife-chopped tabbouleh, the charcoal-grilled lamb and the spiced kebabs made by hand.

We work with day-fresh produce: herbs, milk-fed lamb, market fish and seasonal vegetables. Sauces, pickles and sweets are prepared in our kitchen every morning.

Whole sea bass marinated with garlic, lemon and cumin, opened over charcoal. The smoke perfumes the skin; the flesh stays white, juicy, with a golden crust.
